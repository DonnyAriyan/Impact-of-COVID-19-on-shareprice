COVID-19 And It's Impact on Gross Domestic Product (GDP) of various countries across the globe

It is undeniable that COVID-19 pandemic had presented us with new challenges and posed with not only social disruption but also as a profound risk to the health and growth economies of countries the globe. This is measured by Gross Domestic Product or GDP which, in short, refers to the overall health of the economy of a particular country, measured by the total monetary value of all final goods and services produced and sold in the economy.

GDP per capita for countries across the world

Through observing and interpreting Figure 1, we are able to deduce that the year, 2020 had the biggest hit on the overall economies of the world. Even though the COVID-19 pandemic surfaced in late 2018, countries began to feel the full effects in this year, 2020. Majority of countries experienced a negative percentage (%) change in GDP except for Guyana which experienced a 43.5% increase in its GDP relative to the previous year, 2019.
